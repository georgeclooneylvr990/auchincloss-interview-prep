History & Identity

Once the largest textile-producing center in the United States. By 1920: 100+ mills, 1.8M spindles, 30,000 workers. The American Printing Company (6,000 workers) was the world's largest print cloth producer. The 1920s collapse — competition from low-wage Southern mills — devastated the city. By 1930, 73 mills liquidated. City of Fall River declared bankruptcy. Population has declined from 130,000+ at peak to ~94,000 today. 10th largest city in Massachusetts; designated a state Gateway City.

🇵🇹 Portuguese & Azorean Community — Fall River
~46%
of Fall River population is Portuguese-American

Fall River has the densest concentration of Portuguese-Americans in the United States. A UMass Dartmouth study found 49.6% of the city is of Portuguese descent. Immigration began with the whaling and fishing industries in the 1870s, accelerated with textile mill recruitment, and surged again after the 1957 Capelinhos volcanic eruption on Faial island in the Azores — the U.S. lifted immigration quotas for Portuguese citizens displaced by the disaster.

Community Details

  • Origins: Predominantly Azorean (São Miguel, Faial, Pico islands); some continental Portuguese and Madeirans
  • Columbia Street is the heart of the community
  • Santo Christo Parish (Columbia St, est. 1892) — "Mother Church of the Portuguese parishes in the diocese"
  • Fall River is called "the most Portuguese town in America"
  • Fall River Line Pier still handles cargo from the Azores — maintaining direct economic ties to the homeland
  • Portuguese restaurants, bakeries, social clubs, and cultural festivals throughout the city

💉 Opioid Crisis — Fall River & Southeastern MA

Fall River Overdose Deaths

Context & Response

For a city of ~94,000, these numbers are catastrophic — far exceeding state and national rates. Fentanyl is the dominant substance; xylazine ("tranq") has also entered the supply. Massachusetts has invested $700M+ in substance use prevention and treatment in FY2025.

  • MA DPH distributed 504,000+ fentanyl test strip kits (since 2023)
  • RIZE MA / DPH: $3.75M Mosaic Opioid Recovery Partnership (May 2024) for Fall River and other underserved communities
  • MA Senate (July 2024): legislation enabling needle exchanges, drug screening, overdose prevention facilities statewide
  • DOJ #ResistTheRisk campaign targeting drug trafficking networks in southeastern MA
🏠 Housing Affordability Crisis

The Fall River Problem

MassINC found Fall River has: the most severe housing shortage of any Gateway City in MA; almost the greatest shortage of affordable housing for lowest-income households; and the highest rate of no-cause evictions. Vacancy rate: 1.2%. Rents up 35% (2017–2022); new market-rate leases up 68–88%. A 2BR apartment runs ~$2,041/month in a city where median income is $50,778.

The Inner Suburb Problem

Newton, Brookline, Wellesley, and Needham have median home prices of $1.5M–$2.3M. Teachers, firefighters, nurses, and young professionals cannot afford to live in the communities they serve. Auchincloss's response: championing the MBTA Communities Act (requiring transit-adjacent zoning for multi-family housing) and the Affordable Housing Credit Improvement Act federally.

🌊 Environmental Challenges

PFAS Water Contamination

PFAS ("forever chemicals") contamination of drinking water wells is a district-wide issue — particularly in Mansfield, Milford, Norton, Sharon, Plainville, and other communities with industrial legacy. Auchincloss has made PFAS remediation a signature multi-year earmark priority, putting PFAS treatment projects in every Community Project Funding cycle since 2022.

Other Environmental Issues

  • Taunton River — excess nutrients, bacteria violations at all monitoring sites (2025), stormwater runoff; EPA removed 15,000 cubic yards of chromium-contaminated soils in 1998
  • Fall River Landfill Superfund Site (EPA NPL since 1983) — capped; affected residents given permanent water supply
  • Fall River mill-era brownfields throughout downtown and waterfront
  • EPA 2024: $3.895M for 11 electric school buses + $10M lead service line replacement in Fall River

🇵🇹 Portuguese Community — Fall River & Taunton

  • Myles Standish Industrial Park (Taunton) — 1,029 acres, 8,000+ jobs — is the district's biggest employment hub outside the Route 128 belt
  • South Coast Rail stations (East Taunton, Fall River Depot) are designed to reduce Route 24 commuter traffic pressure and connect workers to Boston without a car

Route 1 Corridor (Foxborough, Mansfield, North Attleborough, Attleboro)

  • Major commercial strip; Gillette Stadium (Foxborough) brings event traffic and hospitality economy
  • Cabot Business Park (Foxborough/Mansfield line) — largest or 2nd largest industrial park in Massachusetts
  • Auchincloss secured $400K for Foxborough sewer design along Route 1 — enabling commercial growth
